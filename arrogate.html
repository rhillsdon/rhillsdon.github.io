Demolition: Arrogate

De_Arrogate is yet another endeavour in level design.

Overview

In the past, while working on countless fruitless side projects, and while working on Drainage, I realized how boring the simple, technical grunt work can be after you get good at more high-level tasks. With that in mind, I enlisted the help of my good friend Conrad Belaire to work on my next CS:GO map. Like me, Conrad has a majorly passionate interest in game design, and level design especially. However, he had never worked with the Source engine before.

We decided that this was a great opportunity for me to have someone to do some of the gruntwork, and he had the opportunity to learn the basics of the Hammer editor (the tool used to edit Source engine maps.) We both worked on the design of the map, and we decided that we would work on areas seperately. This is similar to the technique used in Drainage, as well as other popular community maps such as de_sparity by the ever wonderful 3kliksphilip. This had several advantages and disadvantages. On one hand, creating the actual areas was much faster and easier than before, and we were able to focus on our respective tasks. On the other, this left the areas feeling slightly more disjointed and out of place in regards to each other. With no clear central vision for the map until roughly halfway into production, this made the map feel a little bit artificial, and made the later phases of production a pain.

Luckily, through the use of good texture placement, displacements, props, and some clever lighting tricks, we were able to make the map feel much more cohesive and lively, though the initial separation did leave a lasting mark on the maps overall quality. Games like CS:GO are full of improbably spaces, or unrealistic venues, but in a good map, people won't notice the way it "feels" at all, because it feels "right." The map released January 9th, and I consider it to be a success, but also somewhat of a work in progress as it never reached the same popularity as Drainage, and I feel like it could be improved upon in many ways.
