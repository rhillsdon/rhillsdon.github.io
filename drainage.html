Drainage

De_Rainage is the first map I released publicly, as well as the first CS:GO map I made.

Overview

I've been with the Source engine for a long time. Like any long-time friend, it sometimes takes a few years of semi-probing conversation and awkward get togethers culminating in nothing in particular in order to really get to know someone. For more than 7 years I've been messing with the Source engine, specifically in the Hammer editor. After seeing what you could do with custom maps, I dug a little deeper and found 3kliksphilip's huge repository of tutorials and guides. Not only was this extremely useful in learning, it also served as a huge inspiration in my desire to create games. For years, I dabbled in level creation for various Source games, but finished very little and released nothing. However, once I was in highschool, I remembered what I had done before and thought to myself "now is the time."

Fast forward almost an entire year, and for the first time ever, I publicly released a map to the public. That map is de_rainage, or Drainage. I was a bit rusty, and had never mapped for CS:GO before, so the final result was far from a masterpiece. But damn, was I proud of it. I rounded up a few friends to help test it and provide feedback, and eventually ended up with more than 200 people subscribed to the map. For a first release, it felt very good.

The actual development of the map was very interesting as well. Inspired by eastern-european draining stations, I wanted to add a gimmick seldom used in CS:GO maps: Water. Water is interesting in CS:GO. It slows your movementspeed, defuses Smoke and Incendiary grenades, negates falling damage and it makes any movement much louder. As I was soon to discover, it also doesn't render properly in certain circumstances due to Source engine limitations, and just generally makes development of your map a hellscape of agony and torment. In the end, it all worked out, even if I saw a map (the name eludes me) release on the /r/CSGOMappers subreddit that was also water themed immediately before I finished Drainage. Even though this map might not be known by many, and certainly isn't the next Dust2, I had a lot of fun making it, and I learned a lot from it.
